Visa-Free Entry

Stay: 90 days within 180 days

Citizens from over 95 countries can enter Qatar without a visa and stay for up to 90 days. Entry stamp received at the airport upon arrival.

Requirements:

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Sufficient funds for stay

GCC Citizens

Stay: Unlimited

Citizens of Gulf Cooperation Council countries (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman) can enter and stay in Qatar freely with just their national ID card.

Requirements:

  • Valid national ID card or passport
  • No visa required
  • No time limit on stay

Visa on Arrival

Stay: 30 days (extendable)

Citizens from select countries can obtain a visa upon arrival at Hamad International Airport. Quick processing at immigration counters.

Requirements:

  • Valid passport (6 months minimum)
  • Visa fee payment (QAR 100)
  • Confirmed hotel reservation
  • Return flight ticket

Electronic Visa (e-Visa)

Processing: 3-5 business days

Apply online through Qatar's official visa portal. Most common visa type for tourists who don't qualify for visa-free entry or visa on arrival.

Requirements:

  • Online application form
  • Passport copy (6 months validity)
  • Recent passport-sized photo
  • Hotel reservation confirmation
  • Return flight booking
  • Visa fee (varies by nationality)

Embassy Visa

Processing: 10-15 business days

Required for certain nationalities. Must apply in person or through authorized agent at Qatar Embassy or Consulate in your home country.

Requirements:

  • Completed visa application form
  • Original passport (6 months validity)
  • Two passport photos
  • Hotel booking confirmation
  • Flight itinerary
  • Bank statements (3 months)
  • Travel insurance
  • Visa fee

Transit Visa

Stay: Up to 96 hours

Free transit visa for passengers with layovers in Qatar. Allows you to explore Doha during your connection. Available to most nationalities.

Requirements:

  • Confirmed Qatar Airways ticket
  • Layover between 5-96 hours
  • Valid passport
  • Apply through Qatar Airways website

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I extend my visa-free stay?

Yes, visa-free stays can be extended once for an additional 90 days. Apply through the Ministry of Interior's Metrash2 app or visit their office.

Do children need visas?

Yes, all travelers including children and infants need appropriate visa documentation. The same requirements apply regardless of age.

Can I work on a tourist visa?

No, tourist visas do not permit employment. You need a work visa sponsored by a Qatari employer to work legally in Qatar.

What if my visa application is rejected?

Visa fees are generally non-refundable. You can reapply after addressing the rejection reasons or contact the Qatar embassy for clarification.

Do I need travel insurance?

While not mandatory for most visitors, travel insurance is highly recommended. Some visa categories may require proof of insurance.
