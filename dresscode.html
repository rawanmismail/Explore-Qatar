Dress Code & Etiquette

Your guide to dressing appropriately and behaving respectfully in Qatar

The Golden Rule

"Dress modestly, behave respectfully, and you'll be welcomed warmly."

Qatar is a conservative Islamic country, but it's also cosmopolitan and welcoming to visitors. The key is modesty and respect. You don't need to wear traditional dress, but covering shoulders and knees in public is expected.

üèôÔ∏è Public Places (Souqs, Malls, Streets)

High Modesty Required

‚úÖ Acceptable

  • Loose-fitting tops that cover shoulders
  • Trousers, jeans, or long skirts (below knee)
  • Maxi dresses with sleeves
  • Palazzo pants or wide-leg trousers
  • Cardigans or light jackets over tops
  • Closed-toe or sandals (both fine)
  • Light scarf (optional but useful)

‚ùå Not Acceptable

  • Sleeveless tops or tank tops
  • Short skirts or shorts above knee
  • Tight, body-hugging clothing
  • Low-cut or see-through tops
  • Clothing with offensive slogans
  • Beachwear outside beach areas
  • Crop tops showing midriff

üè® Hotels & Private Venues

Moderate Modesty

‚úÖ Acceptable

  • Sleeveless dresses (knee-length)
  • Casual Western clothing
  • Sundresses with straps
  • Capri pants or bermuda shorts
  • Smart casual evening wear
  • Cocktail dresses (appropriate length)

üí° Tips

  • More relaxed than public areas
  • Still avoid extremely revealing clothing
  • Bring wrap/cardigan for hotel common areas
  • Fine dining = smart casual minimum

üèñÔ∏è Beach & Pool

Relaxed

‚úÖ Acceptable

  • One-piece or two-piece swimsuits
  • Bikinis (at hotel pools/beaches)
  • Swim shorts and tops
  • Cover-up when leaving pool/beach area

‚ùå Not Acceptable

  • Topless sunbathing (illegal)
  • Thong bikinis
  • Walking to/from beach in swimsuit alone
  • Swimwear in hotel lobby or restaurants

üïå Mosques & Religious Sites

Very High Modesty Required

‚úÖ Required

  • Head scarf covering hair (abaya often provided)
  • Long sleeves covering to wrists
  • Long skirt or trousers (ankles covered)
  • Loose-fitting, non-transparent clothing
  • Remove shoes at entrance
  • Socks recommended

üí° Tips

  • Katara Mosque provides abayas for visitors
  • Bring your own scarf for convenience
  • Dark or neutral colors preferred
  • Most mosques not open to non-Muslims

üåô During Ramadan

Extra High Modesty Required

‚úÖ Expected

  • More conservative than usual
  • Shoulders and knees covered at all times
  • Loose clothing preferred
  • Avoid tight or revealing outfits
  • Consider bringing light cardigans
  • Respect the holy month with extra modesty

üí° Tips

  • Hotels are more relaxed but still modest
  • Public areas require extra coverage
  • Local women often wear abayas
  • Visitors not required to wear abaya

ü§ù Social Etiquette

üëã Greetings

General Rule: Polite, respectful greetings are important

  • Say: "As-salamu alaykum" (Peace be upon you)
  • Response: "Wa alaykumu as-salam" (And peace be upon you)
  • Handshakes: Common between same gender
  • Men-Women: Wait for woman to extend hand first; many won't
  • Right Hand: Always use right hand for greetings
  • Eye Contact: Moderate eye contact shows respect
  • Patience: Greetings can be lengthy - don't rush

üíë Public Affection

General Rule: Minimal public displays of affection

  • Acceptable: Married couples holding hands
  • Not Acceptable: Kissing, hugging in public
  • Hotel Lobbies: Still considered public - be modest
  • Hotels Rooms: Complete privacy
  • Unmarried Couples: Extra discretion advised
  • Same-Sex Couples: Any affection forbidden by law

üçΩÔ∏è Dining Etiquette

General Rule: Respect cultural dining customs

  • Right Hand: Eat and pass food with right hand only
  • Hospitality: Accept when offered - refusal can offend
  • Try Everything: At least taste all dishes
  • Shoes Off: Remove if dining on floor
  • Seated Position: Don't show soles of feet
  • Compliments: Praising food appreciated
  • Leftovers: Leaving some food shows satisfaction

üì± Phone & Photography

General Rule: Always ask permission

  • People: Never photograph without asking
  • Women: Especially sensitive - usually decline
  • Mosques: Ask permission, remove shoes
  • Prohibited: Military, police, government buildings
  • Privacy: Respect personal space
  • Social Media: Same rules apply for posting

üè† Home Visits

General Rule: Guest-host relationship sacred

  • Shoes: Always remove at entrance
  • Gifts: Bring dates, sweets, or flowers
  • No Alcohol: Never bring to Muslim homes
  • Accept Hospitality: Declining tea/coffee rude
  • Gender Separation: Men/women may sit separately
  • Compliments: Appreciate hospitality shown
  • Don't Overstay: Watch for cues to leave

üïå Prayer Times

General Rule: Respect prayer times

  • Five Times Daily: Adhan (call to prayer) heard citywide
  • Shop Closures: Some shops close 15-30 minutes
  • Don't Interrupt: Never disturb someone praying
  • Walk Around: Don't walk in front of praying person
  • Lower Volume: Keep noise down during prayer
  • Friday: Holy day - many closures 11am-1pm
